# Where the Light Forgot Me

_by inathi booi_

I don't remember the exact moment the
light stopped showing up for me.

I just know one day, I woke up, and it was
gone.

People always ask, “What happened to
you?" like sadness has a switch, like I can
trace my darkness to one clear explosion.
But it wasn't one thing. It was everything—
too many little breaks in the glass until I
could no longer see the sun through it.
This is not a story of survival. It’s a
confession.

A letter I never had the courage to write
until now.

Because maybe, just maybe, if I tell it all...
the light will find me again.

## Chapter 1 — The Girl Who
Went Quiet

I used to talk a lot. I used to laugh until my
stomach hurt. I used to dream too big and
love too loudly. But people taught me to
shrink.

At home, I learned to keep still. Not
because I wanted to, but because silence
was Safer. The less they noticed me, the
less they could break me. I would sit in
corners, blending into walls, pretending to
be fine.

At school, I became a shadow. People
liked the version of me that smiled on
command and kept her emotions locked in
a cage. They liked me quiet. So I stayed
quiet.

And slowly, I started disappearing from my
own life.

## Chapter 2 — I Wasn't Always
This Cold

Sometimes I look in the mirror and don't
recognize the girl staring back. She looks
numb. Unbothered. Like nothing can shake
her.

But I know her secret. I know she cries
when everyone is asleep. I know she
carries old words like they're written into
her skin. I Know she used to care too much
—until caring started to feel like bleeding.
You don't become cold for no reason.

You learn it. You become it.

Because when you're too soft for too long,
life shows you why that’s dangerous.

I remember the days I begged the sky to
gI've me a sign.

That maybe someone, somewhere, still
cared.

But nothing ever came. And I started to
believe what I feared most:

That this world had no light left for girls
like me.

## Chapter 3 — I Started Writing
to Survive

Nobody noticed I was falling, so I started
writing letters. Not to anyone in particular—
just to the air, to the wind, to the space
between me and the people I couldn't talk
to.

I wrote about the emptiness that kept

showing up uninvited.

About the way I could be surrounded by
people and still feel like I was screaming in
aroom full of silence.

About how tired I was of pretending I was
okay just to keep others comfortable.

And every time I finished a letter, I would
hide it. In library books. Under my bed.
Taped inside the back of an old notebook.
Some part of me hoped someone might
find them. The other part knew no one
would.

But I kept writing anyway. Because writing
was the only thing that felt like breathing
## Chapter 4 — The Day I Broke
Without Making a Sound

It wasn't a big event. No dramatic
breakdown.

It was just a quiet Tuesday, and I couldn't
get out of bed.

The air felt heavy. The walls too close. I
didn't cry—II couldn't.

I just lay there, staring at the ceiling like it
held all the answers.

That’s when I realized...

I had become so used to surviving that I
forgot what it felt like to live.

And maybe that's what this whole story is
about.

Not dying. Not healing.

But remembering. Remembering who I
used to be, before the light forgot me
## Chapter 5 — Home Isn't
Always a Safe Place

People think “home” means comfort.
Warmth. Safety.

But I know different. I know that
sometimes, home is where the pain lives.
At home, I learned how to stay quiet when I
wanted to scream.

I learned how to carry my feelings in
silence, because saying them out loud
didn't change anything—it only made them
worse.

It's weird how a place can have your name
on it, but still not feel like yours.

How you can sit in a room full of your own
blood, and still feel like you don't belong.
Sometimes I'd go into the bathroom, close
the door, and sit on the floor just to feel
alone in peace. Because even the noise of
people who are supposed to love you can
become unbearable when it’s filled with
judgment, pressure, or coldness.

I wanted to leave. Not to run away—just to

breathe somewhere new.

Somewhere the walls didn’t remember
every version of me that broke.
## Chapter 6 — I Started Faking It
Better

There's an art to pretending you're okay.
You smile just right. You laugh at the right
moments. You post things that make it
seem like you're still holding it together.

I became so good at faking it, even I
started to believe it sometimes.

But it’s tiring. Pretending is its own kind of
grief—you're mourning a version of
yourself that never existed, just to survive
the day.

People would say, “You're so strong,’ and
I'd smile, knowing damn well they had no
idea I cried myself to sleep the night

before.

You get tired of carrying yourself like a lie.
But when you don't feel safe enough to be
honest, what choice do you have?
## Chapter 7 — I Started Wanting
Less

I stopped asking for love.

Stopped expecting people to understand
me.

Stopped believing that anyone would ever
choose me and mean it.

It wasn't bitterness—it was exhaustion.

I started to want less. From people. From
life. From myself.

I didn't need big dreams anymore. Just
peace.

Not even happiness—just silence. A

moment where I didn’t feel like the world
was sitting on my chest.

And I know that sounds dramatic, but
when you carry too much for too long,
even breathing feels like work.
## Chapter 8 — When the Light
Showed Up, I Didn't Trust It
Something strange happened.

One day, the light peeked through. Nota
full sunrise—just a soft glow. A moment. A
conversation. A song that made me feel
again.

But I didn't trust it.

Because when you've lived in the dark for
too long, light doesn't feel safe—it feels
SUSPICIOUS.

You start waiting for it to disappear again.
You brace yourself for the crash.

You don't even let yourself enjoy it because
you know nothing good stays.

And maybe that’s the saddest part of all:

I forgot how to hold joy without shaking.
## Chapter 9 — Where It All Began
It all started when I was seven. Not with a
funeral. Not with screams or sirens. Just a
moment. A feeling. A sudden silence
inside me where something used to live.

I lost a part of me that day. And I’ve been
trying to remember what it felt like to be
whole ever since.

Maybe that’s when I forgot how to hold still
without shaking. How to sit ina room
without feeling like I didn't belong there.

How to speak without wondering if anyone
was really listening.

That kind of pain doesn’t need a name. It
doesn't ask for permission. It just... settles
inside you. And grows.

Everyone else kept moving forward—
laughing, living, forgetting. But I stayed
stuck in that seven-year-old body.
Watching people through a glass I couldn't
break.

I didn't know how to Say it then. I still don't,
really. But I think that's the moment the
light forgot me.

## Chapter 10 — The Invisible
Room

Have you ever thought that maybe the
hardest part of pain isn't the pain itself, but

the way it makes you feel so completely
alone—even when you are surrounded by
people?

It’s like existing in an invisible room, one
that no one else sees. You can wave.
Smile. Nod at the right moments. But deep
down, you're hoping someone will see
through the glass.

I tried. I tried to speak, to write, to show it
through my eyes. But pain is tricky—it
teaches you to hide.

So I did. And the more I hid, the more they
thought I was okay. And the more they
thought I was okay, the more I forgot how
to ask for help.

Now I'm eighteen. And I’ve mastered the
art of losing people. Friends. Lovers. Even
parts of myself. Not because I wanted to—
because I didn’t know how to hold on.

My anxiety walks into every room before I
do. My overthinking writes endings to
relationships that haven't even started.
And still, I show up. Even when it hurts.
Even when I want to disappear. Because
deep down, some stubborn piece of me
still believes the light will remember me
one day.

And until then, I write. I bleed on pages. I
try to turn pain into something softer.
Because maybe that’s what healing is—
finding beauty in the places that broke you.
## Chapter 11 — The Day I Faded
in a Crowd

There was this one day. I remember it like
a bruise you keep pressing, just to feel
something.

I was standing in a crowded room. Loud
voices. Laughter. Movement. And I had
never felt more invisible.

I smiled when someone looked at me. I
laughed at the jokes I didn’t hear. I nodded
like I was present—but I wasn't.

I was floating just above the moment,
watching it all unfold like a movie I couldn't
step into.

And it hit me. I could be in a room full of
people and still feel like a ghost.

That was the kind of loneliness no one
talks about. Not the "I have no friends"
kind. The kind where you do have them,
but still feel alone. Still feel misunderstood.
Still feel like no one would notice if you
disappeared.

And in that moment, something inside me
cracked. Not loudly. Not enough for
anyone to hear. Just enough for me to feel
it.

But instead of falling apart in front of
everyone, I walked away. Went to the
bathroom. Shut the door. Sat on the floor.
And I cried without a sound.

Because sometimes, you don't need
comfort. You just need to let the sadness
move through you. You just need to break
without explaining.

That day, I realized: It’s not always the loud
breakdowns that matter. Sometimes it’s
the quiet ones that stay with you the
longest.

## Chapter 12 — When It Rains
Inside

Some days, it feels like it's raining inside
my body. Like there's a storm I carry with
me that no one else can see.

I wake up heavy. Not tired—exhausted.
From the weight of pretending. Of
functioning. Of carrying questions that
don't have answers.

Why do I feel this way? Why can't I just be
happy like everyone else? Why does joy
feel so temporary, and pain feel like home?
I drag myself through the hours like I’m
underwater. Smiling when I should.
Laughing on cue. But everything sounds
distant. Filtered through clouds.

People say, "It'll pass." But what if it
doesn't? What if I've made a home in the
darkness?

What if the light forgot me for real?
Still—I get out of bed. Still—I brush my
teeth. Still—I put on music, even if I don’t
feel it.

Because sometimes survival isn't poetic.
Sometimes it’s just habit. And that has to
be enough.

## Chapter 13 — The Almosts
My life is full of almosts. Almost happy.
Almost healed. Almost loved. Almost
enough.

I almost made it out of that sadness last
year. I almost opened up to that one

person who made me feel seen. I almost
believed I was okay.

But the thing about almosts—they haunt
you. They keep you up at night replaying
what could have been.

Sometimes I wonder if I’m cursed to lI've a
life of nearlies. To always reach, but never
touch.

I meet people who almost stay. I get better
—almost. I let someone in—almost.

But then fear walks in, like it owns the
place. And I go back to being quiet.
Guarded. Alone.

I'm tired of almosts. But they're the only
thing I've known. So maybe I'll learn to live
with them. Until I finally find something
that stays.

## Chapter 14 — The Kindness I
Needed

It wasn't a grand gesture. It never is.

It was just a text: “Hey, I was thinking
about you. Are you okay?”

That message broke something in me. Not
in a painful way. In a freeing way. Like
someone finally opened the window ina
dark room.

It reminded me that sometimes, we
survI've on the smallest pieces of kindness.
A touch on the shoulder. A smile that
lingers. A person who stays when you're
quiet.

That day, I didn’t say much. I didn’t know
how to let people into the mess inside me.

But they didn't leave. They sat there.
Waited. Let me breathe.

And for the first time in a long time, I didn't
feel broken. I felt human.

Not fixed. Not saved. Just... not alone.
And maybe that’s where healing begins.
Not in huge moments. But in the gentle
ones.

## Chapter 15 — The Light Isn't

Some nights, the darkness still whispers.
But now, I whisper back.

I tell it: “I’m still here.”

Not stronger. Not healed. Just still

## Chapter 16 — The Smile I Wear
People say I smile a lot. They think it's a
good thing. They call me strong. Brave. But
they don't know it’s not bravery—it's
survival.

Every smile is stitched with silence. Every
laugh, a distraction. Because if I stopped
smiling, if I let the pain slip out— I might
not stop crying.

So I smile in the mirror to remind myself
I’m still here. I smile for others so they
don't ask questions I can’t answer.

But beneath that smile is a war. A storm I
never chose to fight. And some days, I

don't even know who's winning.
## Chapter 17 — When
Overthinking Becomes a Voice
You ever try to fall asleep, and your
thoughts start sprinting? Like they’ve been
waiting all day for the lights to go off.
“What if they don't actually like you?”
“What if you said too much again?” “What
if you're just... too much?”

My mind talks louder than anyone else in
the room. It replays old conversations.
Writes scripts for moments that haven't
even happened.

It's exhausting. To be at war with your own
head. To want peace, but your brain won't
gI've it to you.

I wish I could turn it off. Even just for a
night.

But until then, I learn to lI've with the noise.
To breathe through the static. To remind
myself that just because my mind says it,
doesn't mean it’s true.

## Chapter 18 — Ghosting Myself
I've ghosted people. But more than that—
I've ghosted parts of me.

The child who used to laugh without
reason. The teen who believed in love. The
version of me that thought I'd be okay by
now.

I left them behind because it was easier
than facing what they became.

But sometimes I hear them. In music. In

memories. In moments of quiet. They're
still there. Waiting.

Maybe one day, I'll meet them again. Not
with shame. But with grace.

“Breathe. You're okay. You're safe.”

## Chapter 21 — Things I Never

Sometimes, I want to scream. Not at
anyone in particular. Just... into the air.
Just to remind myself I still have a voice.

I want to say, “I’m not okay.” I want to say,
“Please stay.” I want to say, “Love me even
when I pull away.”

But I don't. Because I don't want to sound
weak. I don’t want to be a burden.

So I stay silent. And it eats me from the
inside out.

## Chapter 22 — The Room with

Sometimes, it feels like I’m trapped ina

No way out. No way forward.

Just the echo of my own thoughts.

But even then— I find a pen. And I write on
the walls.

Because if I can’t leave the room, maybe I
can change what's inside it.

## Chapter 23 — Quiet Victories
Getting out of bed. Replying to a text.

These are victories. Quiet ones.
Uncelebrated ones. But real.

Some days, survival is the achievement.
And that’s enough.

## Chapter 24 — The Friend Who

Not everyone leaves. Some stay. Some see

There was one. They didn't try to fix me.
They just held space for me.

And that was enough. Sometimes,

## Chapter 25 — If I Could Talk to

Especially in the dark.

## Chapter 26 — Relearning the

The light didn’t forget me. It just got lost

But I see flickers now. In music. In books.
In the way someone says my name softly.
I'm learning to walk toward it again. Even if
I stumble. Even if I’m scared.

Because maybe healing isn't about being
unbroken. Maybe it’s about being brave

## Chapter 27 — Becoming

I'm not who I was. I’m not who I will be. I’m

Somewhere between grief and growth.
Between hurting and healing. Between
forgetting and remembering who I am.

It's messy. It's slow. It’s real.

And it’s mine.
