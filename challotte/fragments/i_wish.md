## Part I: The Confession of the Flame

Oftentimes I sabotaged myself, and I knew exactly why: I wanted peace and clarity away from everyone else around me. I wanted to be alone, far from all this noise and chaos. And so, I self-sabotaged again, stabbing and piercing that knife right through my own heart. Why, though? How could I do it to myself this time?

Life has been great - amazing, even. I've found another way of living. I remember the advice to seek healing through counseling and talking, the warnings about how opening up would free me from myself, and the long discussions about the little things that could trigger the rage and anger I suppressed through all those years of enduring. I know I spoke ill back then, but I am well now. In fact, I feel great. Empowered. I have allowed myself to not only have friends but to truly love and appreciate them. Here we are, feeling lighter than a dandelion floating across a field after a light breeze.

I have about five friends now. They are remarkable individuals in their fields, full of prospect.

There is Natalie, whom I met in my first year at Wits. It turned out we liked almost the same things: chess, brunch, vintage living, and romanticizing life. A little tea party held in a wonderful garden during spring is our favorite excuse to dress up and alter our accents to sound British enough for time travel. She is marvelous. Once, she retook a calculus class just because she didn't get the exact perfect marks she wanted, even though she had already passed. It's the ultimate perfectionist move, and I absolutely loved it. To me, it resembled how free she thinks of herself; she won't let something slide if she has the power to change it.

Then there is Starborn, whom I met around my year of serious rebellion. We had so much fun. Back then, I still thought of myself as daddy's princess, living in a bubble despite an environment that never missed a beat to remind me otherwise. Physically, I was living in a pit hole - deep inside a crusted, rough city, in the heart of a forgotten, decaying building. I hated everything about that place: the dusty hallways, the unreliable elevator, the odd-smelling corridors that made it difficult to escape to my imaginary tea parties in London.There were times I thought about just going on a quest to find the next man willing to take me into his castle and let me feast on his life's work. It wasn't the deed itself that was hard, just the thought of the mental strength and rebellion it would take to achieve it. I failed dismally in each of those mental quests. Maybe it was because of my hot-headedness and my total unwillingness to bend to a man's will. I don't intend to scrutinize my former self too deeply; it would kill my spirit to dissect her like that.

## Part II: The Echoes of Envy

Then there are the others. Starborn's rebound friend - someone I barely knew - evoked resounding feelings in me that pushed me in a contrary direction. Perhaps she reminded me of the relationship I never had with my mother, or the one I wished I had. She unknowingly amplified the parts of myself I had buried instead of facing, the parts I hid instead of letting the sun burn the living hurt out of them. I had to come to terms with the fact that my envious friction had little to do with her; she was just an entity representing the pieces of myself I hadn't tapped into yet.

But that doesn't exempt her from my genuine dislike of how she chose to scrutinize my expression. I remember posting a photo on Instagram - me sitting at a bar with my friend Pixie, wearing a black fitted mesh jumpsuit, a floral scarf framing my face, minimal but seductive makeup, long boots, and a fur coat just out of frame. The mood was warm, cozy, nightlife. She saw it and decided to use it as gossip fuel for Starborn, bashing it, saying I needed to "tone it down, i'm doing too much," that it was "too revealing" and "inappropriate for Instagram." Well, congratulations to the Minister of Moral Dressing. Kudos on becoming the monarchy of social media modesty. I just think if you're going to critique my clothes, you should do it to my face. I am an easy person to critique; I won't cry, and I won't break. I look for the truth in what people say, and I speak my truth back, whether it displeases them or not. I choose what offends me through reasoning and validity. If someone says something hurtful but true, I can calmly say, "I hear you, that makes sense," and move on. But the two-faced energy around her was impossible to ignore.

## Part III: The Poison in the Text

The one I won't mention is slightly astounding. She struck right when I least expected it and used a poison I thought she did not possess. She gave me the best smackdown and ultimate trash on why you should never let your guard down. Talk about returning to campus after a very lovely holiday in Cape Town. It was around dinner time at home. My dad had just finished making our dinner, and we were going to sit down and enjoy that with one of those political debates he likes. As I sat on the couch trying to settle myself comfortably, my phone vibrates… trtrtrtr. It's a message from Starborn. I feel a bit lazy and reluctant to check because I know it will turn into a long conversation of us debriefing the holiday and just having a good laugh. I think to myself, let me enjoy my steak and mash; I will give her a call before I go to bed. My dad comes and gives me my food. Before I could taste another bite, texts come through… trtrtr. Okay, now I have to check, because two texts in less than a minute signal it is really good tea that just cannot wait for the daily debrief hour before bed.I'm on my phone now. As I click to read the message, an astounding, impossible rock hits my chest. The text goes like:

"Hey, have you ever had a fight/altercation with Ponani?"
The next one is just a dying need for a response:
"???"
I reply with a nice:
"Not at all, why?" 
 
She tries texting back and decides no, she has to call for this one. My phone rings, so I get up and make my way to my room with my food, signaling to my dad that I have to take the call. After about an hour of multiple unexpected shocks - bricks and cement of impossible, revolting scenarios and ultimate, disturbing shocks about me - I turn cold. I'm in awe; I'm shocked to the ultimate core. You could tell me the worst shocking news, no matter how awful and sad, and I still wouldn't get past that level of shock. I was cognitively, psychologically, and emotionally paralyzed. Like, what in the living hell was she trying to accomplish?
I switch off my phone, eat my food, and prepare for bed. My whole body and soul were in complete denial, like there is absolutely no way on earth that Starborn was telling it as it is. I had to be in Joburg the next few days to digest this in person. The next few days in Cape Town, I was just out of my body, trying to get past each day. I did think of telling this to someone, maybe my dad, but I don't know how to do that. I never talk to my dad about anything, let alone my mother. Even though my dad is way closer to my heart, I still have never confided in him about anything that was not a joke. 

## Part IV: The Confrontation and the Crisis

My first day in Joburg, I had to have brunch by Bannister, and I saw you walking past. You did not realize my presence in the area, nor did I intend to make you aware. Moments of being glad I'm back and seeing Starborn passed, and I wanted to know exactly what was going on.She was extremely reluctant at first, completely detached from wanting to tell me all this, which puzzled me because when mountains separated us, she jumped quickly on that call. The atmosphere was heavy; I could tell she was weighing the validity of these allegations, and she was also confused and just trying to look out for her best interest. I was not playing either; I wanted to know at any cost.She first says I should ask Ponani myself and that she doesn't want to get in between. I'm taken aback, feeling stripped of my innocence by the second. I make it easy:
"I know you and understand why you are holding back, but I want you to know I don't appreciate it. You can always keep your distance from me to protect yourself if you feel like doing so, but you have to tell me exactly what it is she said about me. Me and you have been friends for so long, and I never gave you any reason to doubt me, nor have you given me one. However, you holding back on telling me to my face everything Ponani had the highest audacity to say about me is not shining a better light on you."
I was hurt, deeply wounded at that point, and I know I was putting pressure on her, but I did not care. Because you know what? If anything was being said about her, I would tell her straight from the moment I heard it. I continued to make it known that anyone who knew about this and did not tell me is not really a true friend to me, and God as my witness, I will unfriend them. I was not joking at all, and I think she also realized that much.

We go to the Freshers party; we are dressed and look all cute. Pixie is also there, and we are pregaming. I am pulled together, and my emotions are all in check. We start at Bannister, take a few shots each, get some really cute photos, and then make our way to the party. It was lit; it was nice and vibey with good music and good people. That was my first time at Freshers, and I was loving every bit of it until… I got drunk. The alcohol kicked in and did its best to amplify my feelings. Now I was in a disinhibited state, and my raw, unprocessed feelings were racing each other to the surface. The raw, unprocessed, and unfiltered Challotte was out. I was mad. Autopilot was over; I was enraged more than I could comprehend. I started weeping, and they later told me that I kept saying, "Why me? Like, how could she do me like that?" I kept rehearsing each and every one of her accusations.

I think I was only doing so in my head, but who knows, I might have said one or two out loud. 
I'm thinking to myself:
1.She claims that her guru told her that she is having insomnia and struggling to focus on her schoolwork and perform her best because someone is after her intellectual property.
2.The person is intensely after her and her life's prospects.
3.The same person has been using a technique called "knowledge swapping," and she has been practicing the aforementioned method for years, and that is how they got through prior stages of their life.
4.The person does not belong at Wits; they lack the cognitive functioning that a Wits candidate needs to get through. The person is not smart or intellectually gifted at all; they are just a fraud who gets by by preying on individuals such as her.
5.She tells Starborn to be extremely cautious around my presence because I am this entity that is assigned by dark powers to perform these dark assignments on her, and therefore Starborn should be vigilant because I am extremely dangerous and witchy.
6.She says because I sometimes watch Shera Seven, the "sprinkle sprinkle" lady, I perform dark magic.
7.She claims now I am after her whole family, and they all cannot live in harmony and peace and be prosperous on earth because I want harm their way.

I go on and on in my head. At this point, I myself am extremely puzzled. Shera Seven talks about a gold-digging lifestyle, so what does that have to do with all this nonsense? If I was able to perform knowledge swapping, wouldn't I just utilize that power by swapping knowledge at the bank and getting richer? I'm perplexed. If I was so powerful as she claims, why would I waste my power on lower forms like that instead of doing worthwhile things, becoming more blessed financially, and having absolutely no need for a degree? If I possessed such power, I wouldn't be using it to swap useless university knowledge I can get on a YouTube video; I would do next-level stuff. 
I realized that I am getting more pissed by each and every one of these allegations because they have no stance whatsoever. She wants to tell me about not belonging at Wits? Missy, I turned down UCT, remember? I got into UCT and you did not. Who did I switch my knowledge with in high school then? Oh no, what about primary school? You go to my mountain collection of awards, medals, and certificates that date back from kindergarten, and you want to say I switched knowledge with a kindergarten kid on how to do my ABCs? 
Now I'm thinking maybe she is envious that she studied all the time and I would go out, come back, and study a semester's worth of work in two days and still be above average. If that was it, did she miss the fact that even then, I was not doing as well as I did in first year? I got above average because I did use to do some studying here and there along the semester, and just because I did not tell her doesn't mean I did not do it. I'm thinking, so for a full 2 to 3 months I used to see her at school, and the times I used to request to shower at her res (which was a communal shower, by the way, when my building did not have power), she was looking at me with dark eyes and thinking I'm there to poison her life's work… I was feeling so shut out. Like, wtf, all these times we all used to chill, she was tiptoeing around me and thinking to herself, "Emihle is so dark, she is the reason I take drugs to sleep now and can't get above average even though I studied." What a bitch! I had to address her.

## Part V: Guarding the Name

I saw her the other day as I was making my way to campus. I had confided in my aunt on the phone the night before and had a very good cry. She reminded me that this has nothing to do with me and I shouldn't doubt myself, because I was starting to do so. I had started doubting my capabilities and why God made me like this. Why do I seem to have people want to treat me well? Why do I seem to make some people at ease and some not at ease? I had started debugging how I do it - how I learn that much content in two days and still pass. Like, is there something I am not aware of that keeps helping me? I had started trying to believe the most shameful things being said about me. I wanted to at least see some tiny bit of truth so I could understand why she would go to such lengths trying to discredit who I am. She made it her mission, so I came to realize, that she had diffused this narrative about me to almost everyone whom I knew that she also knew. Some she knew through me, but she made sure everyone saw me as the biggest villain of all time. Witchcraft is not something black groups take lightly. She nailed the last nail in the coffin when she validated her claims by saying she went to a spiritual leader (a prophet or whatever), and she knew most of the people she told would take it very seriously and be extremely shaken because they were black. Imagine laughing and talking to someone and not realizing that they are being fed the most villainizing poison about you, and they are also just trying not to be your next victim. Imagine walking around and people are thinking such things about you - maybe she's walking that fast to practice her next spell, or look at how she's dressed all in black today, it must be the day of the ritual… A whole 2 to 3 months of some of my mates thinking all that about me, and I couldn't even tell. I was beat, shamed, and ultimately left for dead.

I thought about being the bigger person and just taking it in because I know the truth and time will later show her it was not me. BUT I recalled The 48 Laws of Power, Law 5:
"So much depends on reputation - guard it with your life.''

I had to do something about this. I cannot let her slide. I did a bit of research online and figured she is tarnishing my reputation at this point. Her not liking me is okay, but diffusing such shameful things about me around the people I hang around is an active attempt at ruining my reputation. I walked up to them, and she was with someone. I called her to the side and asked for a quick chat. Talk about extreme pretense when they see me - all smiles and attempting to hug, asking, "How are you?" I was amused at how long she was planning to keep it rolling. I asked her to the side and quickly sum it all up for me: what is it that she has been actively diffusing about me, and why? She couldn't come up with a definite answer. I proceeded by telling her that I am not interested in going back-and-forth with this; let's just do a lie detector test. I told her, "You can find the place, time, and the person, and I am going to cover the costs no matter how much they are." I was very assured in myself at that time, and I wanted her to know that was the last straw. She said things that were not making sense and denied some of the things she supposedly said. I told her, "Sweetheart, if you do not like me, that is okay. I do not have to like you either. But if you want to have this battle with me, let's do it the legal way. Bring the person who said all that to the court, we do some lie detector tests to please everyone, and clear my name from all this so you can know that I am not after you, nor will I ever be." She suddenly became a bit reluctant and focused on denying, and then I proceeded again to let her know that I will sue her for defamation of character should I sense that she has not stopped, and then I left.


Being you is not easy, and maybe that is what forms you the way you are, really. To truly process all of this, I had to do some serious self-reflection, dating it all back from the earlier life moments until now. I wrote something for you just after i learned all this; maybe I thought it would provide some clarity, understanding, and a bit of comfort in such times….

2 days I don't feel

Two days, and I don't feel.
Two days after, and I still don't know.
I don't know what it is about me - 
It's like a torch that won't burn out,
It's like a rose that won't decay,
A route that won't delay.

Persistent, it won't let go.
Dedicated, it won't stop showing.
Consistent, it vowed to be what it's been - 
No motivation needed for it to be.

I tried dimming it so it won't be seen,
Dimmed it again and again so it's dim.
"Dim it for me," I thought, "so they won't see."
Dim it is, but still, they could see - 
A little light across the sea,
Far, but close enough to be seen.

One saw it.
Two conspired about it.
Three… it probably reached more than that.

Just one loyalty it needed:
A person who will believe in it more than I,
A person who will rescue it, not hide it like I.

It reached…
She did.

The thing is, she didn't understand what it is.
Sometimes it poses as a threat, but it's not.
At times it is a mess.
She misjudged it, left my existence for dead.
Because it is mine, she had but one deed:
Consult. Consult me on what it is.

And I could tell her:
It is a Star.

Bury it today, it will live.
Come back tomorrow, it will breathe.
You can't kill it,
Nor dim it.
I tried.
Ask my past offenders - it won't die.
It will live.
It is MyStar.
